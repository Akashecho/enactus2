Overview

Sustaining Agriculture, Empowering Farmers

Project Astitva is a sustainable agricultural initiative that bridges environmental conservation with farmer empowerment. The project focuses on creating high-quality, eco-friendly organic fertilizers using natural waste components like cow dung (Gobar), crop residue, and kitchen compost.

Designed to combat the adverse effects of chemical fertilizers—such as soil degradation, water pollution, and increased farming costs—Astitva offers a natural alternative that restores soil fertility, improves crop yield, and promotes sustainable farming practices.

Eco-Friendly Composting

Transforming agricultural and natural waste into nutrient-rich organic fertilizer without harmful chemicals.

Soil & Health Benefits

Restores soil microbiology, increases water retention, and enables the production of chemical-free, healthier food.

Farmer Empowerment

Reduces dependency on expensive synthetic fertilizers, decreasing production costs and increasing agricultural profitability.

The Core Identity

Behind the Name: Astitva

The word Astitva is a Sanskrit term meaning "Existence" or "Identity". Our project is built on the belief that every individual, regardless of their background, deserves to have a recognized identity and the strength to define their own existence.

By focusing on women's empowerment and youth skill development, Project Astitva aims to transform "hidden existences" into "powerful identities". Through storytelling in our magazines and skill-building in our workshops, we help individuals reclaim their space in society and write their own narratives of independence.
